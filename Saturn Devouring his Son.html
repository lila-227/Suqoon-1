Saturn Devouring His Son

Saturn Devouring His Son is the name of a painting by the Spanish painter Francisco Goya.

The painting depicts the Greek myth of the Titan Cronus, who, according to the myth, fearing that he would be overthrown by his children, eats them all at birth.

Legend says that Saturn received a prophecy that one of his sons would overthrow him and seize power. Fearing the prophecy, he swallowed his children as soon as they were born, devouring them alive in their cradles. Eventually, his wife succeeded in protecting one of the sons by hiding him—Jupiter (the same as Zeus in Greek mythology). Jupiter later grew up, forced his father to release his brothers, and then defeated him.

This painting is one of 14 paintings called the Black Paintings that he painted directly onto the walls of his house sometime between 1819 and 1823 AD.

This painting was transferred to canvas after Goya’s death, and has since hung in the Prado Museum in Madrid.

From my point of view, the painting speaks to the most common thoughts that we, as humans, experience. I will do this today to ensure that this does not happen in the future, but what if it happens even after I have done what does not achieve it now in the past?

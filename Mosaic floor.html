Mosaic floor

The art of mosaic began to spread in the Greek world, then the Romans adopted and developed it until it became an essential part of decorating palaces, villas, baths, and temples.

In Egypt, this art spread after Alexander the Great's conquest in 332 BC, and then flourished during the Ptolemaic and Roman periods. Cities such as Alexandria, Fayoum, and Antinoopolis (modern-day Sheikh Abada village in Minya Governorate) were among the most important centers for mosaic production.

This mosaic fragment was discovered in the Shatby district of Alexandria in 1892 AD. It was used as a floor in the banqueting room of a wealthy person's house in ancient Alexandria. This floor was decorated with floral motifs made from large pieces of colored marble. It is considered one of the oldest known mosaic fragments in Egypt, made during the Ptolemaic period in the 2nd century BC in Alexandria.

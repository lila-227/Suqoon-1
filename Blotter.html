Blotter

The painting "Blotter" is a famous artwork by the Scottish painter Peter Doig, completed in 1993.

The painting is based on a photograph Dweig took of his brother standing on a frozen pond in Canada. The artist pumped water over the ice to enhance the reflections and transform the scene into an introspective reflection.

The word "Blotter" refers to the absorption or merging of a person with nature, and relates to Duig's technique of painting by saturating the canvas with paint. It also carries a subtle reference to "blotting paper" used for LSD, which, according to the artist, reflects a state of delirium, memories, and feelings of loneliness and isolation.

This painting earned Dweig first prize at the John Moores Painting Exhibition in 1993, and paved the way for his nomination for the Turner Prize in 1994. The painting is currently part of the permanent collection at the Walker Art Gallery in Liverpool.

From my perspective as someone who appreciates art, this painting makes you involuntarily immerse yourself in it and reflect. You keep thinking about the emotions it evoked within you, and you drift away into your inner world. In my opinion, this is the artist's aim: before depicting emotions in a painting, he intended to evoke the very emotions he was creating.
